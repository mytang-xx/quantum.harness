Method review · current to 2026-05

Exact diagonalization is the small-system oracle that 2024–2026 quantum many-body work cannot replace.

Density-matrix renormalization group (DMRG), neural quantum states (NQS), variational and quantum Monte Carlo (VMC, QMC), and variational quantum eigensolvers (VQE) absorb most frontier compute, yet every 2024–2026 paper claiming a phase, a scar, a fractional Chern number, or an entanglement spectrum is anchored against an ED point. Three forces shape the present: sublattice coding + space-group irreducible representations push spin-½ ED to N ≈ 50; polynomial-filter and shift-invert solvers carry interior eigenstates to D ≈ 108–1010; GPU and Chapel-based matrix-vector products (matvec) move the frontier sideways, not yet through.

2. The 2026 feasibility ceiling

Practical D by hardware regime. D is the symmetry-reduced sector dimension — not L, not N.

Dense full spectrum, single workstation (256–512 GB RAM)
D ≈ 1.5 × 105
LAPACK dsyevd saturates the node. Single-shot eigh at D ≈ 6 × 104 in seconds; D ≈ 1.5 × 105 in hours.
Dense full spectrum, fat node + MPI (ScaLAPACK / ELPA)
D ≈ 5 × 105 – 106
ELPA 2025.06 ROCm 6.4.2 solve_tridi ~5× faster on AMD. D ≈ 106 on a 32–256 node partition is the current anchor.
Sparse interior eigenstates (Krylov / polynomial filter / shift-invert)
D ≈ 1010 – 1012
XDiag at D ≈ 1010 on thousands of CPU cores; Tianhe MT-3000 1D Hubbard at D = 7.3 × 1011. POLFED at D ≈ 108 on workstation + GPU.
GPU-accelerated sparse Lanczos (single node, H100 / A100 / MI355X)
D ≈ 108 – 109
QISG suite at 236 × 236 on 4 × A100; FuzzifiED CUDA Krylov; POLFED.jl GPU sparse matvec. No fully-distributed multi-GPU ED record as of 2026-05.

4. Tricks list

Twenty-three tricks 2024–2026 ED practice has converged on. Each row: status, tag, one-paragraph explanation, citation, your notes.

Sublattice coding for spin-½ bases at N ≥ 40
Wietek-Läuchli 2018 (arXiv:1804.05028) · XDiag arXiv:2505.02901
symmetrybasis encoding

Partition the lattice into A/B sublattices, store each sublattice's bit-pattern table separately, then resolve the symmetry sector by indexing pairs (a, b). At N = 40–50 the standard 2N-entry representative table is impossible; the sublattice approach replaces it with two 2N/2-sized tables plus a small lookup. This is what makes spin-½ ED at N = 50 feasible at all.

Lin tables for arbitrary symmetry-projected states
H.Q. Lin 1990 · XDiag arXiv:2505.02901 · FuzzifiED arXiv:2503.00100
symmetrybasis encoding

Encode a basis state as a pair (high-bits, low-bits) so representative-finding is O(1) memory access. Remains the workhorse for translation + point-group projection at large N. XDiag (2025) ships the first general implementation that handles arbitrary space-group symmetries on arbitrary lattices.

Constrained-basis enumeration via DFA / transfer-matrix walk
arXiv:2408.14372 (PXP, Fibonacci, avalanche-MBL)
constrained Hilbertbasis encoding

For PXP-like models, the legal Hilbert space is a regular language (Fibonacci words for PXP) generable by a small DFA. Enumerate the constrained basis by walking the DFA; gives D ≈ φL (PXP) or smaller exponents for hand-designed constraints. Sierant–Lewenstein combine this with a genetic search to find constraints of exp(0.28 L) growth, reaching L = 31.

Polynomial-Filter ED (POLFED) on the fly inside Lanczos
arXiv:2005.09534 (method) · arXiv:2605.10191 (Polfed.jl, GPU)
interior spectrumeigensolver

Apply a high-degree polynomial p(H) sharply peaked around a target energy E*, then run Lanczos on p(H) — matrix-free, just K sparse matvecs per outer step. Memory scales as O(D) rather than the O(D × LU-fill) of shift-invert; reaches larger D. Polfed.jl (2026) supports GPU sparse matvec and auto-tunes polynomial degree for structured H.

Shift-invert Lanczos with parallel sparse direct factorization
SciPost Phys. 5, 045 (2018) Pietracaprina · MUMPS, STRUMPACK, SLEPc
interior spectrumeigensolverparallelism

Factor (H − σI) = LU once with MUMPS or STRUMPACK, then run a few Lanczos iterations on the inverse. Memory dominated by LU-fill, so caps at D ≈ 106 even on a fat MPI node — but gives the cleanest interior eigenpairs in fixed iteration count. The reference fallback when POLFED isn't available; still standard for MBL at L ≤ 22.

Chebyshev filter diagonalization (ChebFD) / dual application of Chebyshev polynomials (DACP)
arXiv:1510.04895 (ChebFD) · arXiv:2011.02107 (DACP, 8–30× over shift-invert)
interior spectrumeigensolver

Chebyshev filter projects onto a chosen energy window via K-degree Chebyshev recursion of H — pure matvecs, no factorization. Best when you need 103+ eigenstates near the band center. DACP reports 30× over shift-invert for Ising, 8× for spin glasses. FuzzifiED implements a ChebFD-style filter in its Krylov entry point.

Matrix-free symmetry-reduced matvec with on-the-fly phase factors
XDiag arXiv:2505.02901 · lattice-symmetries arXiv:2104.04011
sparse storagesymmetry

At D > 108 the Hamiltonian as a CSR matrix doesn't fit in RAM. Enumerate non-zeros of each term on the fly, compute the representative and the symmetry-character phase per matrix element, accumulate into the output vector. XDiag, FuzzifiED, lattice-symmetries, Spinpack all implement this. Phase-factor bugs are the most common silent failure mode.

Random-hash basis partition for distributed-memory matvec
XDiag arXiv:2505.02901
parallelismsparse storage

Distribute basis states to MPI ranks by a random hash of their bit-pattern — preserves load balance even when symmetry sectors are unevenly sized, gives O(1) ownership lookup. XDiag's "near-linear scaling on thousands of cores" claim rests on random hashing rather than older row-block partition.

Chapel / one-sided MPI matvec — 7–8× over MPI+OpenMP
Westerhout arXiv:2308.16712 (SC23)
parallelism

One-sided communication for the off-diagonal scatter — basis states owned remotely are fetched without explicit MPI_Sendrecv pairing. 7–8× speedup on 32 nodes / 4096 cores; scales to 32k cores. Lives in lattice-symmetries v2. Chapel runtime adoption outside Cray/HPE installations is the practical blocker.

Energy-variance as the universal Lanczos stop rule
arXiv:2504.21786 · arXiv:2510.17490
eigensolver

For any eigenstate of H, Var(H) = ⟨H²⟩ − ⟨H⟩² = 0. Track the running variance of the candidate Ritz vector; declare convergence when Var(H) drops below the squared spectral resolution needed. Default diagnostic now in MPS-Lanczos and NQS variance-criterion work. Cheap, basis-independent, robust against ghost eigenvalues.

Thick-restart / Krylov-Schur for many simultaneous eigenpairs

Continued-fraction Lanczos vs kernel polynomial method (KPM) Chebyshev expansion for spectral functions
Standard KPM (Weiße-Fehske 2006) · KPNQS 2024–2025

A spectral function A(ω) = −1/π Im⟨0|(ω+iη−H)−1|0⟩ can be evaluated either by continued-fraction Lanczos (K poles → K Lorentzians) or by Chebyshev KPM (δ(ω−H) as Chebyshev polynomials, recursion = K matvecs). KPM gives smoother continuous spectra; continued-fraction is sharper at discrete poles. 2024–2025: KPM ported to NQS (KPNQS) and to quantics tensor cross interpolation.

64-bit indices and basis sizes > 231 — a real failure mode

At D > 2 × 109, 32-bit integer indices overflow. Sparse libraries indexed by int32_t (older cuSPARSE, older ARPACK) silently produce garbage. Fix: compile against int64_t sparse routines (SuiteSparse long, cuSPARSE-12+, MKL _64), or keep matvec matrix-free with size_t everywhere. Q1. XDiag vs HPhi for large 2D spin-½ at L = 36–48. XDiag's sublattice coding + space-group irreps in 2026 is publicly the first such implementation, but HPhi has years of accumulated tuning for matvec throughput on K-class machines. On a current Fugaku partition with full point-group reduction, does XDiag actually beat HPhi on wall time per Lanczos iteration, or only on ergonomics?
Q2. Status of GPU ED in 2026. No front-line tool (XDiag, QuSpin, HPhi, EDIpack2, DanceQ) ships GPU kernels. cuQuantum/Lightning/Qulacs are statevector-only. EDLib has a CUDA PR merging May 2026 but no release. Is anyone running cuSPARSE+cuSOLVER seriously at D > 107 for sparse interior eigenstates today, or is GPU ED still a single-author script affair?
Q3. DanceQ in production for MBL. DanceQ + SLEPc polynomial filter is, on paper, the cleanest interior-spectrum stack for higher-spin MBL. Has anyone actually run PolFED-style calculations at L = 22, S = 1 inside it? How does wall time compare to a hand-rolled POLFED implementation?
Q4. Pomerol vs EDIpack2 in real DMFT pipelines. Pomerol still has the cleanest two-particle Lehmann implementation, but no release since 2023. EDIpack2 added broken-symmetry support but the two-particle GF infrastructure is less mature. For a serious 2026 cluster DMFT (5 orbital + SO + s-wave SC), is anyone still using Pomerol, or has TRIQS+EDIpack2 absorbed all of impurity-ED?
Q5. lattice-symmetries v2 Chapel rewrite. The SC23 result was striking, but no new release in 2024–2025. Is the Chapel runtime mature enough on real HPC sites in 2026 to recommend it over MPI+X, or has the v2 effort effectively stalled?
Q6. SU(2) symmetry in ED. No front-line ED tool handles non-Abelian SU(2) natively in 2026 — surprising given its 4–8× memory savings on Heisenberg. Is there a current group working on a public SU(2)-aware ED code, or is the consensus that the implementation cost isn't worth it given the state of DMRG with SU(2)?
Q7. QuSpin's velocity. v1.0.0 (Oct 2023) → v1.0.1 (Apr 2024) → ~2 years of maintenance commits. With Weinberg in industry and Bukov at MPI-PKS, is there an internal plan for a v1.1 / v2 (GPU / MPI), or is QuSpin in long-term care?
