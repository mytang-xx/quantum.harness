# Calogero–Sutherland (Sutherland) model — exact-solution oracle

Technique: T3 (Bethe ansatz / Yang–Baxter) · Tier: B (integrable) · Script: P

## Continuum conventions

$$ H = -\sum_{i=1}^{N}\frac{\partial^2}{\partial x_i^2} \;+\; \sum_{i<j}\frac{2\lambda(\lambda-1)\,(\pi/L)^2}{\sin^2\!\big(\pi(x_i-x_j)/L\big)}, \qquad \lambda \ge 0 $$

- **Setup:** `N` identical particles on a **ring of circumference `L`** (the trigonometric *Sutherland* member of the Calogero–Sutherland family; the `1/x²`-plus-harmonic *Calogero* member [@Calogero1969] is the `L→∞` confined cousin).
- **Units:** `ħ = 1`, `2m = 1` — the kinetic term is literally `−∂²` and a single-particle momentum `k` contributes `k²` to the energy. In these units the free case (`λ=0` bosons or `λ=1` fermions, where the interaction coefficient `2λ(λ−1)` vanishes) gives `E = Σ_i k_i²` exactly.
- **Coupling:** `λ` is the dimensionless interaction/statistical parameter. `λ=0` free bosons, `λ=1` free spinless fermions, `λ=2` the "√2-statistics" point; the coupling strength grows as `λ(λ−1)`. `λ` is Haldane's fractional-exclusion-statistics parameter `g = λ`.
- **Density:** `n = N/L`; the thermodynamic limit is `N, L → ∞` at fixed `n`. Energy per particle scales as `E/N ∼ n² λ²` (the sole dimensionful combination), the exact limit being `(π²n²/3)λ²` (below). See `.knowledge/conventions.md`.

## Solvability statement

T3 (asymptotic Bethe ansatz): the trigonometric `1/sin²` interaction is integrable and the **entire spectrum is closed-form** [@Sutherland1971]. Every eigenstate carries a Jastrow factor `∏_{i<j}|\sin(\pi(x_i-x_j)/L)|^\lambda` times a symmetric polynomial that is a **Jack polynomial** in the `z_i = e^{2\pi i x_i/L}`, and is labelled by a non-decreasing integer sequence `I_1 ≤ … ≤ I_N`. The pseudo-momenta are `k_i = (2\pi/L)[\,I_i + (\lambda/2)(2i-N-1)\,]` and the energy is `E = Σ_i k_i²` — the `(\lambda/2)(2i-N-1)` term is the asymptotic-Bethe statistical shift. **Tier B, script-P rationale:** the spectrum, ground-state energy and gaps are genuinely closed-form (scripted here, and *exact* — this is unusually strong for a Tier-B card); the **dynamical density-density and one-body Green's functions** are *also* exactly known, at every rational `λ`, from the Jack-polynomial expansion [@Ha1994], but that machinery is **not scripted** — it is tabulated below. Hence P, partial.

## The closed-form-spectrum story

The Jastrow ground state `ψ_0 = ∏_{i<j}|\sin(\pi(x_i-x_j)/L)|^\lambda` is an exact eigenstate: substituting it into `H`, the `sin^{λ-2}` pole terms generated by the kinetic operator cancel the interaction exactly, leaving the eigenvalue

$$ E_0 = \Big(\frac{\pi}{L}\Big)^2\lambda^2\,\frac{N(N^2-1)}{3}, \qquad\text{from}\quad \sum_{i=1}^N\Big[\tfrac{\lambda}{2}(2i-N-1)\Big]^2=\frac{\lambda^2}{4}\cdot\frac{N(N^2-1)}{3}. $$

Excited states shift the quantum numbers `I_i` off zero. Two elementary excitations set the finite-size **gap** `= (2\pi/L)^2\min\!\big(N,\ 1+\lambda(N-1)\big)`:

- a **rigid centre-of-mass boost** `I=(1,\dots,1)`: `ΔE = N(2\pi/L)^2` (total momentum `2\pi N/L`);
- an **internal particle–hole** move (top `I_N\!\to\!1` or bottom `I_1\!\to\!-1`): `ΔE = [1+\lambda(N-1)](2\pi/L)^2`.

The two branches cross exactly at `λ=1`, where both equal `N(2\pi/L)^2` — the free-fermion particle–hole gap. For `λ>1` the boost is cheaper; for `λ<1` the internal move is. At `λ=1` the whole construction reduces to a Slater determinant of plane waves (the Jastrow becomes the Vandermonde), and `E_0` equals the free-fermion Fermi-sea energy `Σ_{filled}k^2` with `k=2\pi m/L` — a closed-shell sum for `N` odd. This exact reduction is anchor (i) of the self-test.

## Exact results

- Ground-state energy `E_0 = (\pi/L)^2\lambda^2\,N(N^2-1)/3` (Jastrow GS) [@Sutherland1971]
- Full spectrum `E(\{I\}) = Σ_i (2\pi/L)^2[I_i+(\lambda/2)(2i-N-1)]^2`, `I_1≤…≤I_N∈\mathbb{Z}` [@Sutherland1971]
- First-excited gap `(2\pi/L)^2\min(N,\,1+\lambda(N-1))` (CM-boost / particle–hole branches)
- `λ=1` reduction: `E_0` equals the free-fermion `Σ_{filled}(2\pi m/L)^2`; `λ=0` free bosons (`E_0=0`)
- Thermodynamic energy per particle `E_0/N → (\pi^2 n^2/3)\lambda^2` (`n=N/L`, `N→∞`)
- **Dynamical correlators (exact, not scripted):** density-density `⟨ρρ⟩` and one-body Green's function have closed Jack-polynomial forms at every rational `λ`; the dynamical structure factor has sharp support between `p`- and `h`-branch thresholds — fractional exclusion statistics `g=λ` [@Ha1994] (tabulated; needs Jack-polynomial machinery, out of script scope)

Self-test anchors: (i) **λ=1 free fermions** — `e0(N,L,1)` equals the directly summed Fermi-sea energy `Σ_{filled}(2\pi m/L)^2` for `N∈{3,5,7}` to `1e-12`; (ii) **N=2 relative motion** — the exact two-body GS energy `2λ^2(\pi/L)^2` (relative coordinate is Pöschl–Teller-on-a-ring, `ψ_0^{rel}=|\sin(\pi x/L)|^\lambda`, `E_{rel}=2λ^2(\pi/L)^2`, CM at rest) is reproduced for several `λ`, and the pinned value `e0(2,2\pi,2)=2` exactly; (iii) **large-N scaling** — the closed form's own `N→∞` leading term `(\pi/L)^2λ^2N^2/3` equals `(\pi^2 n^2/3)λ^2` (`n=N/L`) to `1e-10` at `N=10^4`, and the full `e0/N` sits within the `O(1/N^2)` finite-size correction; (iv) **gap** — `first_gap` matches brute-force enumeration over `I` across `N∈{2..5}`, `λ∈{0.3,…,3.5}` (both branches) to `1e-9`; (v) `λ=1` gap `= N(2\pi/L)^2`.

## Verification recipes

- To check an ED/DMRG/first-quantized run of the Sutherland ring at coupling `λ`: build `H` exactly as above (mind the `(\pi/L)^2` in the interaction and the `2m=1` kinetic normalisation) and compare the ground energy to `oracle.py --N … --L … --lam …`'s `e0` to machine precision — this is closed form, so any mismatch is a convention slip (a factor `2m`, a `(2\pi/L)` vs `(\pi/L)`, or `λ(λ−1)` vs `λ²`).
- To check an excited level or a gap: reconstruct it from `energy_from_I(I,…)` with the appropriate `I`-sequence; the smallest nonzero excitation is `first_gap` (CM-boost for `λ>1`, particle–hole for `λ<1`).
- To check a thermodynamic-limit code: `E_0/N` must approach `(\pi^2 n^2/3)λ^2` from below as `1/N^2` — the `−1` in `N^2−1` is the exact finite-size correction, not a bug.
- To check a dynamical-correlator code (structure factor `S(q,ω)`, Green's function): the exact Jack-polynomial results of [@Ha1994] apply at rational `λ`; the support of `S(q,ω)` is bounded by the `p`- and `h`-excitation thresholds with statistics parameter `g=λ` (not scripted here).
